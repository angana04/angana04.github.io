Background

I’m a senior at San Diego State University majoring in Management Information Systems. I was born and raised in India, a country that thrives on diversity, innovation, and resilience. Growing up in a fast-changing environment taught me to adapt quickly, stay curious, and find creative ways to solve problems.

Moving to the United States at the age of seventeen for college opened my perspective even more, helping me understand how technology and data connect people across different cultures and industries.

A Little About My Home

India has always been more than just where I grew up, it’s a part of who I am. The country’s diversity, colors, traditions, and energy constantly inspire me to stay open-minded and curious. Growing up surrounded by such a mix of cultures taught me the importance of community, empathy, and creativity. No matter where I go, the values I learned in India continue to guide how I think, work, and connect with others.

A little fun fact about my hometown, Ahmedabad- Narendra Modi Stadium, the largest in the world, is located here!

It is also recongnized for it's extravagant celebration for the festival of Navratri!

Purpose

I want to use data and technology to make a difference. To me, analytics isn’t just about numbers. It’s about uncovering insights that help people and organizations make smarter, more human-centered decisions. Whether it’s improving a business process, enhancing user experience, or telling a story through data, I’m driven by the idea of turning information into action.

What I'm Doing Now

I’m currently interning at HERC Jobs, where I assist in managing and analyzing higher education recruitment data. My work involves identifying hiring trends, improving data accuracy, and supporting initiatives that promote diversity and inclusion in academic hiring.

This experience has helped me grow as a problem solver while reinforcing my interest in data-driven decision-making and social impact.
